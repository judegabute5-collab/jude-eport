My School Events

A showcase of the events, activities, and experiences that have shaped my academic journey and personal growth.

MATH MONTH

During Math Month, I joined different activities with my classmates. I joined the Damath competition and finished in 4th place. Even though I did not win, the experience taught me to think fast and stay focused during the game. I felt proud because I still did my best during the competition.

I also participated in the Math Mural activity. Our section worked together to create a mural for the competition between sections in our batch. We shared ideas, helped each other, and tried to make our mural creative and meaningful. The activity showed our teamwork and effort as a class.

Overall, Math Month brought fun and excitement to our section. My classmates and I enjoyed the activities and supported each other. The event helped us appreciate math while spending time together as a class.

ENGLISH MONTH

English Month felt memorable for me because our section worked hard together. One of our biggest achievements was the door book cover competition. We designed our classroom door based on a chosen book. Everyone helped with ideas and decorations. When our section won, I felt proud because our effort as a class paid off.

I also supported our class representative in the Booklandia competition. I served as a backdrop during the performance. My role was small, but I still felt happy because I helped our class and showed support for my classmates. It felt nice to be part of something where everyone worked together.

We also had classroom activities like poster making and slogan making. I joined these, but I did not do well in them. Even though I felt a bit disappointed, I still saw it as a chance to learn. English Month taught me that winning feels good, but trying, supporting others, and learning from mistakes also matter.

MOST VALUABLE PERSON (MVP)

The MVP event felt meaningful for our section because everyone showed strong support for our representative. As a class, we cheered for them and helped in any way we could. Our representative joined the competition and won a title, which made our whole section proud.

We also worked together to create the sash for our representative. We spent time planning the design and making it look good. Our effort paid off because the sash also won Best Sash. Seeing our work recognized made us feel happy and proud of what we achieved as a team.

For me, this event showed the good values of our section. We showed teamwork, support, and unity. We also prayed and hoped for the best, and it felt like our prayers were answered. The whole experience reminded me that success feels more special when everyone works together and supports one another.

MITING DE ADVANCE

Miting de Advance helped me understand the importance of leadership and responsible voting inside the school. During the event, the candidates presented their platforms, goals, and plans for the students. I listened carefully to their speeches and observed how they expressed their ideas and promises. This event showed how student leaders guide activities and represent the voice of the student body. It helped me see how leadership requires confidence, clear plans, and the willingness to serve others.

I apply this lesson by becoming more aware of how I choose leaders and by paying attention to their plans and actions. I also show participation by listening respectfully and supporting fair elections in school. If I explain this to a classmate, I describe Miting de Advance as an event where candidates present their ideas before the election so students know who deserves their vote. This event holds value because it promotes student participation, responsible decision making, and awareness about leadership within the school community.

BATTLE OF THE BANDS

Battle of the Bands showed the value of teamwork, dedication, and musical talent among students. Different bands performed songs in front of the school community, and each group displayed their effort through practice and coordination. I watched the performances and saw how the members worked together to create strong and entertaining music. Their confidence on stage showed the result of preparation and cooperation. The event created an atmosphere where students appreciated music and talent.

This lesson applies to real life through teamwork and discipline. Strong results appear when people cooperate and practice their skills. If I explain this to a classmate, I describe Battle of the Bands as a competition where student bands perform and judges select the best group based on musical performance. The school benefits from this event because it gives students a chance to express their talents, gain confidence, and support one another through music.
